This article is based on research in the Netherlands into the private as well as the public sector, describes ten dilemmas likely to be encountered in the management of reorganizations. They may be viewed as the primary choices to be made with respect to the decision-making topic, as tools or levers with which management can attempt to keep control of the process. Specific practical circumstances will usually rule out some of these choices. A good manager will take this into account in his approach, and will thus start with an analysis of the situation. This article aims to offer some assistance on this point: on the one hand, with a description of the most relevant situational factors, and on the other hand, with a flow chart that can be used in designing one's own approach.
