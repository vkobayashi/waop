After a century and a half of thriving development, the Tayloristic production concept is now past its prime and a new concept is evolving: flow-oriented production. Personnel management is rooted in the Tayloristic concept and has to be revisited before it can join the changing conditions and contribute to these fundamental changes. This main line of reasoning is followed on the basis of a sociotechnical perspective. This argument is developed into three main issues, namely: the functional orientation of personnel management; the fact that personnel management is a functional department itself; and finally, the functional organization within personnel departments.
